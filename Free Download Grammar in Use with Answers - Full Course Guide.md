## Common Grammar Mistakes to Avoid

Being aware of common grammar mistakes can help you avoid making them in your own writing and speaking. Here are a few examples:

*   **Incorrect Verb Tense Usage:** Using the wrong verb tense can change the meaning of your sentence. Pay attention to the context and choose the appropriate tense.
*   **Subject-Verb Agreement Errors:** The verb in a sentence must agree with its subject in number. If the subject is singular, the verb must be singular; if the subject is plural, the verb must be plural.
*   **Misplaced Modifiers:** A misplaced modifier is a word or phrase that is placed incorrectly in a sentence, causing confusion about what it is modifying.
*   **Pronoun Agreement Errors:** A pronoun must agree with its antecedent (the noun it refers to) in number and gender.
*   **Incorrect Use of Prepositions:** Prepositions can be tricky, as their usage often depends on context and idiom.
